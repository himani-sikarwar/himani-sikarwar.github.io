Vehicular ad-hoc networks (VANETs) inflict smart traffic control using information exchange (i.e., warning messages, routing and network discovery messages) with nearby vehicles immediately in a highly dynamic environment. Prior approaches have proposed several routing computations and discovery techniques using prediction, direction aware, and hello message assisted algorithms. However, most of them do not perform adequately in real-life traffic situations and have various concerns i.e., broadcast storming, unpredictable network dynamics, fixed-route life-time problem, non-linear real-world traffic, high routing overhead, and low packet delivery ratio.
